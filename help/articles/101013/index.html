Reporting Content

Information regarding reporting user created content on Hydraulisc & How-To


Why reporting content is important

Please note that whilst we try our very best to keep Hydraulisc clean and safe to use for everyone, we can't accomplish that alone. There will always be someone, somewhere, trying to break the rules.

That's why we introduced Content Reporting, which allows users to report content that is potentially inappropriate.

There are penalties for abusing the reporting system!

We try our very best to try and keep Hydraulisc clean and safe for everyone. That said, we cannot accomplish that goal alone.

That's where you, our loyal users, come in. By reporting content, we can detect and remove potentially inappropriate content from the platform faster.

We have a Zero Tolerance policy for any kinds of 18+/sexually explicit (adult) contents.

We do not allow for inappropriate content in Profile Pictures, Posts (Images or Text Descriptions), Usernames, Banners, User Biographies, external links or sexually explicit Private Messages, including 18+ Adult Contents, Sexually Explicit Contents (including Images in Posts, Private Messages and Banners, Text Descriptions of Posts or Private Messages). In addition, we do not allow images, references or discussions of Illegal Substances.


How To Report

From within the Hydraulisc Client:

The Desktop Client (Web & PWA):

To report a Post from the Hydraulisc Desktop Clients, follow these steps:

Reporting Posts:

  1. From the post, either the post page or on the homepage, click on the Dropdown Menu.
    2. (spoiler, click to reveal)

    Sample image with a post on the Hydraulisc Desktop Client showing the icon.

  2. Click on the "Report Post" option.
    3. (spoiler, click to reveal)

    Sample image with a post on the Hydraulisc Desktop Client showing the "Report Post" option in the Dropdown Menu

  3. You're all set. The post has been reported, and our Team will look into it.

The mobile Client (Web & PWA):

Currently, the reporting system is not natively supported on mobile Web & PWA clients.


Through our Discord Server:

We don't currently have a Tickets system in our Discord, so please ping Ami (@sleepingami) and request to DM regarding the platform (for anonymous reporting. Feel free to mention it is regarding content on Hydraulisc you want to report if you wish to make your report public).

What data you will be asked for:

  • Is it an Account or Post you are reporting?
  • Provide links and/or ID's.
  • Reason for the report.

That's pretty much it. Please note that neither Ami nor the Hydraulisc Team in general will get back to you if you reach out to report this way.

Reporting Accounts:

The Desktop Client (Web & PWA):

To report an account from the Hydraulisc Desktop Clients, follow these steps:

  1. From the Users Account Page, click on the Dropdown Menu.
  2. Choose "Report Account".
  3. You're all set! The account has been reported. Our Team will look into the report thoroughly.

The mobile Client (Web & PWA):

Currently, the reporting system is not natively supported on the mobile PWA client.

Reporting Private Messages (PM's)

To report a Private Message on Hydraulisc, follow these steps:

On the Desktop Clients:

  1. Right-click the message from the chat you wish to report. Alternatively, you can left-click and hold, simulating a long-press on mobile.
  2. Click on the "Report Message" option from the Dropdown Menu.
  3. Follow the walkthrough, making sure you read it carefully.
  4. You are all set. Please note that as stated in the walkthrough, the content of the message you reported will be sent, unencrypted, to our Review Team, along with the ID of the message, message chat and the user who sent the message.

On the Mobile Clients:

  1. Long press on the message you wish to report.
  2. Press on the "Report Message" option from the Dropdown Menu.
  3. Follow the walkthrough, making sure you read it carefully.
  4. You are all set. Please note that as stated in the walkthrough, the content of the message you reported will be sent, unencrypted, to our Team, along with the ID of the message, message chat and the user who sent the message.

Content Reporting is ALWAYS ANONYMOUS. We only log the author credentials in case we find the content to be in violation of our Terms, Privacy Policy, Best Practices or Guidelines and we only log your credentials to prevent abuse. The only user credentials logged are User ID's.
